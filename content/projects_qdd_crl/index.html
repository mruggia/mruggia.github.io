The project didn't end up materializing, but I still greatly value the people at that lab and the talks we had. Before splitting up I worked with them for 3 months as a research assistant, mainly on systems identification of quasi-direct drive actuators, as a sort-of preliminary test for the PhD project.

Quasi-direct drive actuators are motors typically used in legged robots. They are equipped with a low reduction gearbox, which makes them strong but still backdriveable. This is important as it enables compliance and torque-control of the leg joints. What I wanted to find out was: How good is this torque-control actually? Turns out not very good, but still good enough for these robots to work surprisingly well. Below I've posted the plots of 5 of the tests.
