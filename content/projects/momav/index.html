MOMAV Omnidirectional Drone (2022-2023)

Created: 28.7.2023, Updated: 2.11.2025

MOMAV (Marco's Omnidirectional Micro Aerial Vehicle) is my own version of an omnidirectional drone, meaning one that can independently translate and rotate. I see it as an evolution of the omnidirectional drones built by the Autonomous Systems Lab at ETH ZÃ¼rich, with whom I did my master thesis. This project was entirely self-funded (thanks in big part to my family) and executed in my free time after I left the lab. Down below is a manuscript describing the novel aspects of the drone, as well as various test flight results.

At first I tried to publish the manuscript at the Robotics Science and Systems RSS 2024 conference, but it was rejected as one anonymous reviewer found that the advantages of my novel control allocation method were not clearly shown. The remaining two reviewers wrote their final reviews directly to the editor and I'm told by the conference organizers that I can't see those. Next I tried to publish with the journal Elsevier Robotics and Autonomous Systems. They ended up accepting the manuscript on the 31 July 2025 after a long 350 days wait, with only minor precisations. This marks my first publication in a major journal as a first (and only) author.

Paper:  MOMAV: A highly symmetrical fully-actuated multirotor drone using optimizing control allocation
Codebase: github.com/mruggia/momav
