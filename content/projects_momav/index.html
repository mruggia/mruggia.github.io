Marco's Omnidirectional Micro Aerial Vehicle, MOMAV (2022-2023)

MOMAV is my own version of an omnidirectional drone, meaning one that can independently translate and rotate. I see it as an evolution of the omnidirectional drones built by the Autonomous Systems Lab at ETH ZÃ¼rich, with whom I did my master thesis. This project was entirely self-funded (thanks in big part to my family) and executed in my free time after I left the lab. Down below is a manuscript describing the novel aspects of the drone, as well as various test results. This manuscript was intended for the Robotics Science and Systems RSS 2024 conference, but it was rejected as one anonymous reviewer found that the advantages of the novel control allocation method were not clearly shown. The remaining two reviewers wrote their final reviews directly to the editor and I'm told by the conference organizers that I can't see those.
